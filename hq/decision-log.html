🧠 Decision Log

Major Backyard Chicken Planner decisions, why we made them, and what benefit we expect.

📌 Major Decisions

ID Decision Reason Expected Benefit
DEC-001 Use GitHub Pages as the primary hosting platform Free, reliable, simple, and sufficient for a static content/tool site Keep costs low while building proof of concept
DEC-002 Build shared CSS instead of styling every page individually Repeated page styling was becoming inefficient Consistent appearance and faster page creation
DEC-003 Create reusable navigation and footer components Navigation and footer changes should not require editing every page manually Easier maintenance across the full site
DEC-004 Use a Product System with products.js and product-loader.js Affiliate cards were becoming repetitive and difficult to maintain across multiple pages Single source of truth for product recommendations and affiliate links
DEC-005 Use generic product categories instead of brand-specific headings Product recommendations may change over time as better options or affiliate programs become available More evergreen content and easier product swaps
DEC-006 Organize content into authority clusters Google appears to reward topical authority and internal structure Stronger SEO growth and clearer reader journeys
DEC-007 Create BCP Headquarters as a private internal command center The project had outgrown a simple checklist or single dashboard Better planning, prioritization, and long-term project management
DEC-008 Use Product IDs, Project IDs, and future Guide/Calculator IDs Large content systems need stable references Easier tracking, linking, dashboard management, and future automation
DEC-009 Strengthen pages Google is already testing before always creating new pages Search Console data showed certain pages already gaining impressions Improve ranking potential and avoid wasting effort on unsupported topics
DEC-010 Build pages as polished Version 1.0 assets instead of rough drafts that require repeated rework Too much time was being spent going back and retrofitting earlier pages More efficient publishing and fewer repeated revisions

🧭 Decision Rule

Before making a major architecture or content decision, ask:

  • Will this save time later?
  • Will this make the site easier to maintain?
  • Will this strengthen SEO or user experience?
  • Will this reduce repeated work?
  • Will this still make sense when the site has 100+ pages?

If the answer is yes to most of these, the decision is probably worth documenting.
