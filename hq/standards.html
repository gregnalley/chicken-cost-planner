📖 BCP Standards Manual

The operating rules for building, organizing, and maintaining Backyard Chicken Planner.

🏗 Core Build Philosophy

  • Build systems before building dozens of pages.
  • Use one source of truth whenever possible.
  • Make pages polished Version 1.0 assets, not rough drafts.
  • Use Search Console data to guide improvements.
  • Helpful content comes before product promotion.
  • Every page should belong to a content cluster.
  • Every page should give the reader a clear next step.

📦 Product Card Standard

Product recommendations should be managed through assets/data/products.js and displayed using scripts/product-loader.js.

To insert a product card, use: 

<div data-product="PRD-001"></div>
  • Use generic product categories in page headings when possible.
  • Do not overuse product cards on informational pages.
  • Product recommendations should solve a real reader problem.
  • Amazon links can be replaced later with better affiliate programs.
  • Update the product once in products.js instead of editing every page.

📚 Content Cluster Standard

Every new article should strengthen one of the main content clusters.

Cluster Purpose
Chicken Breeds Help readers choose chickens
Baby Chicks Help new owners raise chicks successfully
Chicken Health Help readers identify, prevent, and respond to health issues
Eggs & Incubation Help readers manage eggs, laying, incubation, and hatching
Chicken Supplies Help readers choose useful equipment
Coops Help readers build, buy, size, and improve coops
Predator Protection Help readers protect their flock
DIY Projects Provide hands-on project builds
Calculators Provide interactive planning tools

🧱 Standard Page Blueprint

  1. SEO title
  2. Meta description
  3. Article schema or FAQ schema when appropriate
  4. Banner intro
  5. Quick answer section
  6. Main educational content
  7. Comparison table or chart when useful
  8. Detailed sections
  9. Common mistakes or warnings
  10. Related products only when natural
  11. Related calculators, guides, or projects
  12. FAQ section
  13. Final thoughts / next step

🔗 Internal Linking Rules

  • Every new guide should link to at least three related pages when possible.
  • Hub pages should link to all major supporting pages in their cluster.
  • Supporting pages should link back to the hub.
  • Buyer’s guides should link to related educational guides.
  • Project pages should link to products, guides, and calculators.
  • Do not add random internal links; every link should help the reader.
