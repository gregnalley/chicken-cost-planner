🌾 What to Feed Baby Chicks

Baby chicks grow quickly, and the right feed matters. This guide explains chick starter feed, water, grit, treats, and what not to feed young chicks.

Simple Beginner Rule

For most first-time chick owners, the safest and simplest approach is to feed a quality chick starter feed and keep clean water available at all times. Do not start with adult layer feed.

✅ Quick Answer: What Do Baby Chicks Eat?

Baby chicks should eat chick starter feed. Starter feeds are formulated for fast-growing young chickens and commonly contain about 18–20% protein. Oregon State Extension notes that newly hatched chicks are typically fed starter diet until about six weeks old. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

After starter stage Grower or starter/grower feed Depends on feed brand and flock plan

🐥 Why Chicks Need Chick Starter Feed

Baby chicks are not miniature adult hens. They are rapidly growing birds that need feed designed for growth, feather development, bone development, and energy. Chick starter feed is made in small crumbles or mash that chicks can pick up and eat more easily.

Adult layer feed is formulated for hens that are producing eggs. It contains higher calcium for eggshell production, which is not what young growing chicks need as their main diet.

🚫 Why Baby Chicks Should Not Eat Layer Feed

Layer feed is meant for laying hens, not baby chicks. Poultry Extension notes that growing chickens have a lower calcium requirement and too much calcium can adversely affect their kidneys. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Feed Type Designed For Use for Baby Chicks?
Chick starter Young growing chicks Yes
Starter/grower Growing chicks and juveniles Often yes, depending on label
Layer feed Egg-laying hens No, not as a main feed for baby chicks
Scratch grains Treat/supplemental grain No, not as a main diet

💧 Water Is Just as Important as Feed

Chicks should always have access to clean water. When you bring chicks home, gently dip each chick’s beak into the waterer so it learns where to drink.

  • Use a chick-safe waterer, not a deep open bowl.
  • Clean the waterer often.
  • Keep bedding dry around the waterer.
  • Raise the waterer slightly as chicks grow to reduce bedding contamination.

🪨 Do Baby Chicks Need Grit?

If chicks are eating only commercial chick starter feed, they usually do not need extra grit because the feed is already prepared for them. If chicks begin eating treats, grass, bugs, scratch grains, or other foods, they may need chick-sized grit to help grind and digest those foods.

Poultry Extension explains that chickens need grit to grind and digest grains because they do not have teeth, and that commercial grit is available in chick or hen size. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Important

Oyster shell is not grit. Oyster shell is a calcium supplement for laying hens and should not be used as grit for chicks.

🍓 Can Baby Chicks Have Treats?

Treats are not necessary for baby chicks. For beginners, it is usually better to let chicks focus on starter feed during the early brooder stage. If you introduce treats later, keep them small, occasional, and appropriate for the chick’s age.

Treat Question Beginner Answer

🚫 Foods to Avoid Giving Baby Chicks

Baby chicks are small, and their diet should stay simple. Avoid feeding anything moldy, spoiled, salty, sugary, greasy, or difficult for chicks to eat safely.

  • Adult layer feed as the main diet
  • Moldy or spoiled food
  • Large hard chunks they cannot safely eat
  • Too many treats
  • Salty processed foods
  • Anything that replaces proper chick starter feed

❌ Common Baby Chick Feeding Mistakes

  1. Using adult layer feed too early. Chicks need feed made for young growing birds.
  2. Giving too many treats. Starter feed should be the main diet.
  3. Forgetting grit when feeding treats. Chicks may need chick-sized grit for non-feed foods.
  4. Letting water get dirty. Dirty water discourages drinking and creates brooder problems.
  5. Using a feeder that spills constantly. Feed waste can make the brooder messy and expensive.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best feed for baby chicks?

A quality chick starter feed is the best basic feed for baby chicks. It is designed for young growing birds.

Can baby chicks eat layer feed?

Baby chicks should not eat adult layer feed as their main diet because it is formulated for laying hens, not growing chicks.

How long do chicks eat starter feed?

Many chicks eat starter feed for about the first six weeks, though some products are starter/grower feeds meant to be used longer. Always follow the feed label.

Do baby chicks need grit?

Chicks eating only commercial starter feed usually do not need extra grit. If they eat treats, grass, bugs, or grains, chick-sized grit may be needed.

Can baby chicks have treats?

Treats are not necessary. If used, they should be occasional, age-appropriate, and not a replacement for chick starter feed.

🏁 Final Thoughts

Feeding baby chicks does not need to be complicated. Start with chick starter feed, keep clean water available, avoid adult layer feed, and keep treats limited. Once your chicks grow, you can adjust their feed according to age, flock goals, and the feed label.
